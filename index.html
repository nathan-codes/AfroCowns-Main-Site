Dr Afua Animwaa Asante Twumasi

Keynote Speaker

Dr Afua Animwaa Asante Twumasi, 50 years old (1st February 1974). I have been married for 23 years. I have four amazing girls. I am a Public Health Physician Specialist with a speciality in family Reproductive Health. I have worked for twenty-three years as a medical doctor. I am a public health specialist with expertise in family Reproductive Health. I have headed two polyclinics, Madina Polyclinic and Adabraka Polyclinic, and worked as the Municipal Director of Health Services at Ghana Health Service for thirteen years. I am a National facilitator for Infection Prevention and Control for Ghana Health Service. I am also a Regional facilitator for Quality Improvement and Supportive Supervision for Ghana Health Service Greater Accra. I teach at the Ghana College of Physicians and Surgeons. I love God and love working with youth. I support my husband in working with the youth and young Adults in the Church. I also teach the underprivileged teen class in my church. I love evangelism and head the medical outreach team at Grace Baptist Church. I am also a bible studies teacher of the youth. This I do with my husband passionately. I am passionate about the things of God, family and Health.

Stacey Nzingha Hil

Guest Speaker

Stacey Nzingha Hil, a Chicago native, holds degrees in Political Science and African-American Studies. As an educator, she's co-founded influential organizations like the Chicago Grassroots Curriculum Taskforce. Her teaching career spans Chicago and Philadelphia, where she focuses on curriculum development. Stacey has led professional developments for educational institutions and participated in global programs, including in West Africa. Currently, she serves as the Curriculum Coordinator for the American Institute of Resource and Human Development Fulbright-Hays Group Projects Abroad Curriculum Development Program and contributes to the School District of Philadelphia’s Social Studies Curriculum Development team. Balancing her professional commitments with parenthood, she takes pride in her daughter's career as a kindergarten teacher.

Rachael Sealy

Featuring Special Guest Speaker

UKafrolista is a platform created by Rachael Sealy, 36 London,UK, that celebrates beauty, plus size fashion and womens empowerment via social media and events. Rachael believes in equality for all and encourages open and honest conversations to help spark a change within the current beauty narrative. Rachael is known for being very direct and honest with her audience and aims to educate, inform and empower her followers. Rachael started her content creation career during her fashion university degree, writing about current fashion trends and pop culture. Due to high interest in her natural hair, Rachael started a Youtube channel in 2009 to create exposure for the UK natural hair scene.

Nannete Atuahene

Speaker

Nannette Atuahene was born and raised in the United States, to a mother and father from Kumasi, Ghana. She splits the year, living between Ghana and California. She is a real estate investor and broker for over 20 years, and founder of On Our Watch Global, LLC, a leadership development and orgazational effectiveness consultancy. She also contributes her time and expertise to the community on the streets of Ghana, and to several Ghanaian NGOs, primarily dealing with empowering youth.

Angela

Speaker

Angela began her loc journey when her late husband was terminally ill. At the time it was more about convenience as she had less and less time to deal with her hair but also wanted a style that was healthy and beautiful. As a locked sister, the journey has gone from a need for convenience to being an authentic statement for her 3 daughters who also embrace the beautiful natural hair. Embracing the varied styles that come with it, Angela is content in sharing her loc journey in both her professional and personal life and hope to see more women of color embrace their natural and diverse beauty.
